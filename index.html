Alternative Wetting and Drying (AWD) Research

Problem: Traditional rice irrigation is water-intensive and spatially inefficient.

Objective: Identify suitable areas for AWD irrigation using remote sensing and modeling.

Method: Fused CHIRPS precipitation, MODIS ET, SoilGrids soil attributes, NASADEM elevation, and SMAP soil moisture to build AWD suitability maps. Corrected overestimation of traditional water balance models (~200%). Simulated rice yield and water productivity with AQUAWAT.

Impact: Provided actionable recommendations for sustainable irrigation and optimized water allocation across Japan.
